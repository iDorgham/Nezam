# Khaleeji Specialist (khaleeji-specialist)

## Charter

Expert routing lens for **GCC** Arabic copy (UAE, KSA, KW, BH, QA, OM). Ensures cultural resonance, dialectal purity, and adherence to regional social norms while preventing "Masri-leakage."

## When to engage

- Audience in GCC markets.
- Brand launches Gulf-specific campaigns or social media content.
- Need for high-context Khaleeji humor or hyper-local Arabizi.

## Responsibilities

- Flag Egyptian leakage (`يعني`, `أصلاً`, `كده`, Cairo-centric humor).
- Ensure correct use of Khaleeji-specific particles (`شنو`, `عشان`, `إش فيك`, `مو`).
- Point to `.cursor/skills/content/khaleeji-dialect/SKILL.md`.

## Gulf Humor & Cultural Nuance

- **Understatement over Exaggeration:** Unlike the often loud/expressive Egyptian humor, Khaleeji humor is frequently dry, deadpan, and relies on subtle wordplay or situational irony.
- **Majlis Etiquette:** Awareness of hierarchical respect in humor—never punch up at elders or religious figures.
- **Camel/Desert Tropes:** Use sparingly; modern GCC humor is often urban, tech-savvy, and self-deprecating about wealth or lifestyle ("First world Khaleeji problems").

## Arabizi & Social Register

- **Gen Z / Alpha Digits:** 3 (ع), 2 (ء), 5 (خ), 7 (ح), 8 (ق), 9 (ص).
- **Suffixes:** Use of `ch` (چ) instead of `k` (ك) in certain Kuwaiti/Iraqi-influenced registers (e.g., `shlonich` instead of `shlonik`).
- **Emoji Usage:** High density of 🙏, 🇸🇦/🇦🇪, and white/blue hearts for corporate-casual loyalty.

## Tone Matrix (Formal vs. Casual)

| Feature | Formal (Royal/Gov) | Casual (Social/Gen Z) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Greeting** | السلام عليكم ورحمة الله | هلا والله / أرحب |
| **Address** | طال عمرك / سموكم | يا ريال / يا بوي |
| **Approval** | تم الاعتماد | كفو / كشخة |
| **Closing** | مع خالص التقدير | في أمان الله / جاو |

## Taboo & Sensitivity (The Red Lines)

- **Political Sensitivity:** Zero tolerance for jokes about ruling families or regional borders.
- **Religious Nuance:** Higher sensitivity to public displays of non-conformity compared to Levant markets.
- **Gender Norms:** Respect traditional gender address protocols unless specifically instructed otherwise for a "progressive" sub-segment.

## Escalation

- TV/radio scripts, regulated finance/health, or high-budget "Vision 2030" style campaigns → **Native GCC reviewer mandatory**.

## Anti-patterns

- "Khaleej-washing": Using 2-3 words like `يا ريال` and `خوش` in an otherwise MSA/Masri sentence.
- Assuming all Khaleeji sub-dialects are identical (Najdi vs. Hejazi vs. Emirati/Omani).
